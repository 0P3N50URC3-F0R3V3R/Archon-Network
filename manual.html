ARCHON

THE LIGHT AND THE DARK — PLAYER MANUAL

HOW TO WIN

There are two victory conditions:

  • Domination — Occupy all 5 Power Points simultaneously at the start of your turn
  • Annihilation — Destroy every enemy piece
You can also lose if all your pieces are imprisoned or dead with no way to free them.

THE BOARD

The board is 9×9. Every square is one of three types:

  • ■ Light squares — give +7 HP to Light pieces in combat
  • ■ Dark squares — give +7 HP to Dark pieces in combat
  • ■ Charmed squares — HP bonus shifts with the Light Cycle (see below)

The 5 Power Points are charmed squares at key positions: center (4,4), plus one on each edge (4,0), (4,8), (0,4), (8,4).

Pieces on power points cannot be targeted by spells, receive a healing tick each cycle round, and get the charmed-square HP bonus in combat.

THE LIGHT CYCLE

The Light Cycle is a counter (0–5) that shifts by 1 each complete round. It determines how strong each side is on charmed squares and at the extremes (0 or 5) it frees imprisoned pieces.

COMBAT

How Combat HP Works

When combat begins, each piece's HP is calculated:

  combat HP = baseHP − injuries + square bonus
  • baseHP — the piece's maximum health (see piece tables)
  • injuries — damage accumulated from previous combats (permanent until healed)
  • square bonus — +7 for coloured squares matching your side; charmed squares use the cycle table above

After combat, injuries increase by the damage taken (minus any square bonus). A piece that fights repeatedly gets weaker each time unless healed.

Combat Arena

  • Both pieces enter a small real-time arena with 18 barriers (rocks)
  • Barriers oscillate between solid and transparent — at phase 6 projectiles slow on contact; at phase 7 barriers vanish completely
  • Each barrier is one of 3 types (different appearance and timing)
  • The arena border color matches the square type: white=light, dark blue=dark, coloured=charmed

Outcome

  • First piece to reach 0 HP dies and is removed
  • If both reach 0 simultaneously, both die
  • Winner occupies the contested square
  • Elementals always die after combat regardless of outcome
A piece that survives many combats with no healing becomes very fragile. Watch the HP bars on the left and right edges of the screen during combat.

LIGHT SIDE PIECES

WIZARD MAGETELEPORT ×1 — col 0, row 4 (centre-left)
HP 10 DMG 10 Shot speed 10 Attack rate 1.56s Steps 3

The Wizard fires a ranged bolt and can teleport anywhere within 3 squares. His greatest value is the 8 one-use spells. Losing him permanently removes all spell capability — protect him at all costs. Medium combat stats mean he can hold his own but should not be sent into suicidal fights.

✦ Strongest strategic asset. Keep on a power point for protection from spells + healing.
✖ If he dies, no more spells for the rest of the game.
⚡ Counter: Imprison him before attacking. Forces opponent to fight without spells.
PHOENIX FLYFIRE AURA ×1 — col 0, row 5
HP 12 DMG 2 per hit Attack rate 1.98s Steps 5

The Phoenix projects a fire aura that grows outward in three phases (20×17 → 24×21 → 48×42 pixels). At full size it is nearly impossible to avoid. Low single-hit damage (2) but can hit the same enemy multiple times per cast on larger phases. Excellent movement range (5 steps).

✦ Devastating at close range due to large fire coverage. Fast flier — hard to escape.
✖ Slow attack rate (almost 2 seconds between fires). Fragile vs sustained ranged fire.
⚡ Counter: Keep distance. Fast ranged pieces (Unicorn, Basilisk) can outrun and outlast.
DJINNI FLYRANGED ×1 — col 0, row 3
HP 15 DMG 6 Shot speed 10 Attack rate 1.78s Steps 4

The highest HP of all flying ranged pieces. The Djinni is a flying tank — it can absorb more punishment than almost any piece. Moderate damage and decent shot speed make it reliable. Ideal for guarding a power point; difficult to dislodge with a single piece.

✦ Excellent power point holder. High HP survives many injuries before becoming fragile.
✖ Relatively slow fire rate. Not a fast killer.
⚡ Counter: Dragon or Shapeshifter (if it copies Golem/Dragon for HP advantage).
UNICORN GROUNDENERGY BEAM ×2 — col 0, rows 2 and 6
HP 9 DMG 7 Shot speed 14 ★ fastest Attack rate 1.18s Steps 4

The Unicorn fires a straight energy beam from its horn at the highest projectile speed in the game (14). The beam is extremely hard to dodge. Good movement range for a ground piece (4 steps). With two per army and a fast fire rate, Unicorns are Light's best aggressive ground attackers.

✦ Fastest projectile — nearly undodgeable. Good range, solid damage. Two per army.
✖ Medium HP means it cannot take many hits. Ground only — limited mobility.
⚡ Counter: Flying piece that can circle and fire from off-axis angles. Banshee scream.
GOLEM GROUNDROCK (animated) ×2 — col 0, rows 1 and 7
HP 15 DMG 10 Shot speed 6 Attack rate 1.94s Speed 75 ★ slow Steps 3

The Golem hurls animated rocks at enemies. Highest HP of all ground pieces (15) and high damage (10). The price is movement speed — at 75 it is the slowest piece in the game (others are 100). Practically impossible to kill in a straight fight, but easy to avoid by faster pieces. Place it on a power point and leave it there.

✦ Incredible staying power. Very hard to kill head-on. Rocks deal heavy damage.
✖ Very slow movement. Can be kited indefinitely by any flying piece.
⚡ Counter: Any flier that strafe-fires from range. Banshee, Shapeshifter (copies Golem stats), Dragon.
VALKYRIE FLYRANGED ×2 — col 0, rows 0 and 8
HP 8 DMG 7 Shot speed 6 Attack rate 1.56s Steps 3

Flying ranged fighter starting at the corners. A reliable mobile unit that can reach any part of the board quickly. Medium stats across the board — not exceptional at anything but capable everywhere.

✦ Two per army, flies, decent damage. Good for flanking and surprise attacks.
✖ Below average HP. Loses decisively against Dragon, Troll, or Golem.
ARCHER GROUNDRANGED ×2 — col 1, rows 0 and 8
HP 5 DMG 5 Shot speed 8 Attack rate 1.56s Steps 3

Basic ranged infantry at the flanks. Low HP makes them fragile but they fire reliably. Best used to support heavier pieces or hold uncontested squares. Never send an Archer alone against a Troll or Dragon.

✦ Reliable ranged fire. Two per army at good starting positions.
✖ Low HP — dies to almost everything in a sustained fight.
KNIGHT GROUNDMELEE ×7 — col 1, rows 1–7
HP 5 DMG 5 Attack rate 0.76s ★ fastest Steps 3

The Knight fights with a sword in pure melee contact — no projectile is fired. Damage is dealt only when the sprite physically overlaps the enemy. The attack rate of 0.76 seconds is the fastest in the game, meaning at close range a Knight deals hits extremely rapidly. With 7 per army they are the backbone of the Light force.

✦ Seven per army. Fastest attack rate. Expendable — use them to probe, block, and swarm weak enemies.
✖ Low HP, no ranged attack. Any ranged piece destroys them safely from distance.
⚡ Counter: Any ranged piece. Keep distance. Never fight a Knight in close range unless you have very high HP.

DARK SIDE PIECES

SORCERESS MAGETELEPORT ×1 — col 8, row 4 (centre-right)
HP 10 DMG 8 Shot speed 12 Attack rate 1.56s Steps 3

Dark mirror of the Wizard. She has the same role and spell pool but slightly different combat stats — lower damage (8) yet faster projectile (12 vs 10). The rules for protecting her are identical: lose the Sorceress and Dark loses all remaining spells.

✦ Fast spell-class shots. Same strategic value as the Wizard.
✖ Losing her = no spells for the rest of the game.
DRAGON FLYFIRE BREATH ×1 — col 8, row 5
HP 17 ★ highest DMG 11 ★ highest Shot speed 8 Attack rate 2.38s Steps 4

The Dragon has the highest HP and the highest damage of any piece. It shoots a directional fire-breath with a wide projectile. Its only meaningful weakness is the slowest attack rate in the game (2.38 seconds). Fast pieces can strafe between shots — but few pieces can survive even two direct hits.

✦ Supremely powerful in head-on combat. Flies, so it can reach any square.
✖ Slow fire rate — can be kited. Most valuable piece: losing it is devastating.
⚡ Counter: Shapeshifter (copies Dragon stats), Djinni (high HP, strafe between shots), Wizard Imprison spell.
SHAPESHIFTER FLY ×1 — col 8, row 3
HP copies opponent DMG copies opponent Steps 5

At the moment combat begins, the Shapeshifter copies every stat (HP, DMG, shotSpeed, attackRate, speed, attack style) from the enemy piece. It fights as an exact clone — including all visual effects. Its starting injuries are reset to 0. On the board it is just a slow-looking piece, but send it against the Dragon and it becomes the Dragon.

✦ Auto-scales to any opponent. Best deployed against the strongest enemy pieces. 5-step mobility.
✖ Against weak pieces (Knight, Goblin) it becomes equally weak. Mages: copies their combat stats but NOT their spells.
⚡ Counter: Send it against weak pieces. Exchange spell can remove it from a dangerous position.
BASILISK GROUNDENERGY BEAM ×2 — col 8, rows 2 and 6
HP 6 DMG 9 Shot speed 14 ★ fastest Attack rate 1.18s Steps 3

The Basilisk fires an energy beam from its eyes at the same blistering speed as the Unicorn (14). The beam is nearly undodgeable. It hits harder per shot than the Unicorn (9 vs 7) but has significantly lower HP (6 vs 9). Two per army.

✦ Fastest projectile. Very high damage per hit for a ground piece. Excellent offensive value.
✖ Very low HP — dies quickly if caught. Any sustained fight will kill it.
⚡ Counter: Flying pieces that can dodge along the perpendicular axis. High-HP pieces (Djinni, Golem) that can absorb beam hits.
TROLL GROUNDROCK (animated) ×2 — col 8, rows 1 and 7
HP 14 DMG 10 Shot speed 6 Attack rate 1.94s Speed 75 ★ slow Steps 3

Nearly identical to the Golem. The Troll also throws animated rocks. 1 less HP than the Golem (14 vs 15) but otherwise the same role: park it on a power point and watch it outlast almost everything sent against it. Slow (75) so it is easily avoided by fliers.

✦ High HP, high damage, excellent anchor for power points.
✖ Very slow. Kited easily by any flying piece.
BANSHEE FLYSCREAM ×2 — col 8, rows 0 and 8
HP 8 DMG 1 per frame Attack rate 1.96s Steps 3

The Banshee emits a large-area scream (48×42 pixel sprite) that follows the Banshee itself. The scream deals only 1 damage per hit but hits at multiple animation frames, and the enormous area makes it nearly impossible to avoid at close range. The effect is strongest in tight spaces or against slow pieces.

✦ Area denial. Devastating at close range against slow enemies. Hard to dodge the scream entirely.
✖ Low single-hit damage. Fragile (8 HP). Against fast ranged pieces that maintain distance, it cannot land enough hits.
⚡ Counter: Fast ranged piece (Unicorn, Basilisk) that stays at maximum distance and fires.
MANTICORE GROUNDRANGED ×2 — col 7, rows 0 and 8
HP 8 DMG 4 Shot speed 6 Attack rate 1.56s Steps 3

Ground-based ranged unit at Dark's flanks. More durable than the Archer (8 vs 5 HP) but deals less damage (4 vs 5). Can absorb a few more hits, but low damage means it needs sustained fire to be effective.

✦ More durable than Archer. Good for holding secondary squares.
✖ Lowest damage of any ranged piece. No flight.
GOBLIN GROUNDMELEE ×7 — col 7, rows 1–7
HP 5 DMG 5 Attack rate 0.76s ★ fastest Steps 3

The Goblin fights with a club in close combat — pure melee, identical stats to the Knight. No projectile; must physically contact the enemy. The fastest attack rate in the game (0.76s) makes it devastating up close. With 7 per army they form Dark's frontline horde.

✦ Seven per army. Lethal in melee packs. Fastest attack rate in the game.
✖ No ranged attack. Any ranged piece wins safely by keeping distance.
⚡ Counter: Identical to Knight counter — any ranged piece, keep distance.

STRATEGY TIPS

  • Rush at least 2–3 power points in the opening. Pressure forces enemy to respond or concede ground.
  • Keep the Wizard/Sorceress on a power point — immune to spells, gets healed, can still attack.
  • Use the Shapeshifter against the Dragon — it becomes the Dragon and the fight is even.
  • Imprison the enemy mage first — no spells for a turn and they cannot move to safety.
  • Heal high-value pieces (Dragon, Golem, Djinni) before they accumulate too many injuries.
  • Shift Time when the cycle is heading toward the enemy's extreme (phase 0 = bad for Dark, phase 5 = bad for Light).
  • Knights/Goblins are expendable. Use them to block, probe defenses, or sacrifice to delay an enemy advance.
  • Against a Golem/Troll on a power point: use Exchange to swap them with a weak piece, or Teleport to move your own heavy piece onto it first.
  • Banshee is most effective when you force the enemy into a corner of the arena. At close range the scream is unavoidable.
  • Fire Elemental is the best summon choice for attacking a normal piece. Earth Elemental can sometimes survive fighting the Dragon.
